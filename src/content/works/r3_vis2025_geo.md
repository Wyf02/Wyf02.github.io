## Abstract

This research investigates how geovisualizations can be designed to express and evoke emotional connections between people and places. We propose "affective geovisualization" as a design approach that prioritizes emotional resonance alongside geographic accuracy.

## Motivation

Traditional geovisualizations typically remain emotionally neutral, privileging objective representation. However, many geographic experiences are inherently emotional—belonging, loss, nostalgia, aspiration. This work explores how design can bridge this gap.

## Key Concepts

### Affective Geovisualization
A design paradigm that:
- Acknowledges emotional dimensions of place
- Uses visual language to convey feeling and meaning
- Maintains geographic integrity while adding interpretive layers

### People-Place Relationships
- Sense of belonging
- Historical memory
- Cultural identity
- Environmental stewardship

## Design Study

We developed three prototype geovisualizations exploring:
1. Memory mapping of family histories
2. Cultural heritage visualization
3. Environmental emotion in urban spaces

## User Evaluation

Participants reported:
- Stronger engagement compared to traditional maps
- Ability to perceive new dimensions of familiar places
- Desire to share and discuss these visualizations

## Design Implications

1. **Layering**: Separate geographic and emotional dimensions for clarity
2. **Annotation**: Allow users to contribute their own experiences
3. **Transparency**: Make design choices explicit
4. **Inclusivity**: Represent diverse perspectives and experiences

## Conclusion

Affective geovisualization opens new possibilities for understanding how we relate to space, and demonstrates that emotion and accuracy can coexist in cartographic design.
